# Comparative Analysis

## Objective

To compare the dextral and sinistral dia2 alleles of *Lymnaea stagnalis* and investigate how a single mutation affects protein structure and shell coiling.

---

## Sequence Comparison

The dextral allele encodes a complete Dia2 protein consisting of 1040 amino acids.

The sinistral allele contains a one-base deletion that causes a frameshift mutation. As a result, translation terminates prematurely, producing a truncated protein of only 42 amino acids.

---

## Protein Domain Analysis

The full-length dextral Dia2 protein contains three major functional domains:

- GBD (GTPase-binding domain)
- FH3 domain
- FH2 domain

The truncated sinistral protein lacks all of these domains because translation stops before they can be synthesized.

---

## Biological Interpretation

The loss of functional domains prevents the Dia2 protein from carrying out its normal role in regulating the actin cytoskeleton.

As a consequence, the early embryonic cytoskeletal dynamics required for dextral shell coiling are disrupted, resulting in sinistral shell coiling.
